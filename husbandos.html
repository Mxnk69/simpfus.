His actions catalyze several major events, and his rivalry with Gojo, whom he once deeply respected, adds emotional depth to his storyline. Guts is driven, resilient, and battle-hardened, embodying an intense will to survive despite overwhelming odds. His life has been shaped by constant violence, but beneath his tough exterior lies a vulnerable soul marked by suffering, loss, and loneliness. Guts initially has a solitary mindset, believing strength and self-reliance are his only ways to endure the harshness of life. However, his relationships, especially with Casca and the Band of the Hawk, reveal a softer side, showing he is capable of deep loyalty and love. After his betrayal by Griffith, Guts is consumed by revenge but gradually learns to balance his vengeance with his desire to protect those he cares about. Guts is one of the most skilled fighters in the Berserk universe, displaying exceptional strength, speed, and combat prowess: Swordsmanship: Guts wields the Dragon Slayer, a massive sword that most people could barely lift, with ease and skill. His strength and endurance are almost inhuman, allowing him to take on monstrous foes. Berserker Armor: This mystical armor enhances Guts’ strength and senses, allowing him to fight far beyond normal limits but at the cost of severe strain on his body and mind, often driving him to near madness. Survival Skills: Due to his harsh life, Guts is adept at surviving in any situation, from wilderness survival to taking on large groups of enemies alone. Prosthetic Arm with Cannon and Crossbow: Guts’ prosthetic arm has built-in weapons, making it both versatile and deadly in close-range combat. Guts’ journey is central to Berserk, exploring his struggles with revenge, survival, and the quest for a meaningful life amidst chaos. His story is one of transformation: while he begins as a vengeance-driven warrior, he gradually becomes a protector, balancing his brutal nature with compassion. His battles with the Apostles, Griffith, and his own inner demons are symbolic of the broader themes in Berserk of human resilience and the search for identity and freedom. Guts’ complex journey, filled with pain, determination, and fleeting moments of happiness, makes him one of the most iconic and multi-dimensional characters in manga and anime. His story is a harrowing but inspiring tale of struggle and survival, capturing the enduring fight against overwhelming darkness.

Yuta’s story is one of self-discovery, overcoming trauma, and finding purpose. His growth from a sorrowful, isolated boy into a courageous protector exemplifies Jujutsu Kaisen's themes of resilience, connection, and the complexities of living with supernatural abilities. His abilities and compassionate nature make him a beloved character in the series.

In his current form, Sukuna manifests within Yuji Itadori’s body, and he appears as a tattooed version of Yuji with distinctive marks on his face, symbolizing his cursed nature. Despite his cruel nature, Sukuna is highly intelligent and calculative, often showing an unsettling level of patience and cunning in his plans. His relationship with Yuji, whom he inhabits, is antagonistic yet complex, as he manipulates and tests Yuji while secretly pursuing his own mysterious goals. Sukuna’s pride and ego make him an intimidating figure, as he views most challenges with disdain, showing interest only when faced with particularly strong opponents or unique circumstances. Sukuna is one of the strongest cursed beings in the series, possessing numerous formidable abilities: Vast Cursed Energy: Sukuna has immense reserves of cursed energy, far exceeding most other cursed spirits and sorcerers. His aura alone can induce fear and dread in even experienced sorcerers. Innate Techniques: Sukuna has several cursed techniques, though his full abilities are still unfolding. His known techniques include the Dismantle and Cleave attacks, which allow him to slice through anything in his path with precision. Domain Expansion - Malevolent Shrine: Sukuna’s domain, the Malevolent Shrine, is a powerful and deadly technique that traps his opponent within a vast, shrine-like area where he can precisely target and dismantle anything within range. Regeneration and Healing: Due to his curse energy, Sukuna can heal injuries, even those inflicted on others, making him nearly impossible to kill or permanently harm. Binding Vows: Sukuna forms Binding Vows with Yuji to create deals or limits that benefit him, allowing him to exert control over Yuji’s body for brief periods. Due to his cruelty and strength, he was worshipped and feared as the "King of Curses." These fingers became powerful cursed objects scattered throughout the world, feared by sorcerers and sought after by curses. Sukuna’s power and reputation have endured through the ages, and he is regarded as a mythical being of unparalleled strength, feared even in the present. When Yuji Itadori consumes one of Sukuna’s fingers, Sukuna is resurrected in part, gaining a second life through Yuji and becoming a threat to both sorcerers and curses alike. Sukuna’s presence within Yuji introduces a unique internal struggle, as he embodies the darkness and corruption that Yuji must resist. His relationship with Yuji is symbiotic yet volatile, as Sukuna often takes advantage of situations to further his own interests, frequently testing Yuji’s resolve. Sukuna's goals are largely unknown, though he shows a consistent interest in chaos and an enigmatic curiosity toward certain individuals, such as Satoru Gojo and Megumi Fushiguro. Sukuna’s character serves as a representation of humanity’s darker desires and the allure of power, bringing complex moral and existential themes into Jujutsu Kaisen. His monstrous strength, cunning nature, and unpredictable goals make him an embodiment of fear and tension in the series, serving as both an antagonist and a catalyst for Yuji’s development.
